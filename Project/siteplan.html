The Big Five: African Wildlife Site Plan

Ethan Bray | WDD 131

Site Name

The Big Five Hub

I chose this name because it clearly identifies the niche focus of the website. It positions the site as a central resource for anyone wanting to learn the "why" and "how" behind these iconic animals.

Site Purpose

The purpose of this site is to educate visitors on the history and biological details of the African Big Five (Lion, Leopard, Rhinoceros, Elephant, and Cape Buffalo). It serves as a bridge between South African wildlife heritage and a global audience, providing deep dives into diets, descriptions, and the origin of the "Big Five" term.

Scenarios

  • Why is the Rhino included in the Big Five but not the Giraffe?
  • What are the specific dietary differences between the African Elephant and the Cape Buffalo?

Color Schema

The palette is inspired by the African Savanna—earthy tones with high-contrast accents.

Primary: #2D3047 (Dark Navy/Slate) - Headers & Nav
Secondary: #E07A5F (Terra Cotta) - Accents & Buttons
Accent: #F4F1DE (Cream) - Backgrounds

Typography

Heading Font: 'Playfair Display' (Serif) - To give a classic, "National Geographic" field-guide feel.

Body Font: 'Roboto' (Sans-serif) - For high readability on facts and descriptions.
